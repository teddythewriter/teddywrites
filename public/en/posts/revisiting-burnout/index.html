Revisiting Burnout

Teddy

snippet

288 

2025-02-11 05:30 +0530

I Hate Thinking

I always have. It drags me down a strange, twisting rollercoaster of emotions. No safety bar, no map, just a plunge into the unknown. I always end up in the same dark corner of my mind. A place crowded with what ifs, should haves, and why nots.

And I hate it there.

It’s noisy, uncertain, and heavy.

Still, no matter how hard I try to avoid it, I always find my way back. Sometimes, I go there without even realizing it. Just a quiet moment, a breath too long, a silence too wide. And suddenly, I’m spiraling again.

Work helps. Staying busy helps. Tasks, to-do lists, distractions, They build a dam against the flood. I stack my hours with noise, with purpose, with urgency. But even that has its limits. Eventually, the cracks show.

I can’t keep patching up the holes with productivity. I know what burnout feels like. It creeps in quietly at first, disguised as fatigue. Then it digs deeper, draining everything—motivation, joy, even the will to keep pretending.

Right now, I feel like I’m balancing on a thin, fragile line. One foot in survival, one foot in surrender. I’m not okay, but I’m not gone. Not yet.

Some days, I imagine disappearing. Just slipping away. No grand exit. No explanation. Quietly. Like a whisper no one notices until the silence becomes too loud.

I think about what it would mean to let go. Not in a dramatic way, but in a slow unraveling. A soft release. A break from the noise inside my head. A chance to breathe without fear of drowning in thought.

Maybe that’s what I’m really hoping for. Not escape… just peace. A version of quiet that doesn’t hurt.
